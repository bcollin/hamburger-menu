Simple hamburger menu for generic applications

A bad menu is a bad menu. Sometimes however customers just want a menu without first going into tens of thousands of euros worth of strategy sessions.

Also, by the time I get involved it is generally too late to change the IAD and I have to deliver a solution that is better than 'no solution'. A hamburger menu for mobile applications should suffice in those cases (the 'no solution' solution in those cases just being a long list of links at the top of the page that the mobile user will have to scroll past on every page.

There is no design pattern for this. The name 'hamburger menu' creates the fallacious impression that we by definition are talking about a menu. As far as I am concerned however, site builders can put whatever they want on their hamburgers.

Default: the 'no solution' solution.

Mobile and Javascript? A part of the document gets hidden and replaced by the image of a hamburger.

Desktop: the 'no solution' solution.

For desktop you could choose to enforce the hamburger nevertheless.

Application

Step 1. Make sure from your web page is mobile ready (i.e. viewport is set and whatever).

Step 2. Link the script containing the hamburger menu code.

Step 3. Copy the sample styles to your style sheet and edit to taste.

Step 4. Call hamburger() with the ID of the hamburger element.
